\1. Act on, 2. Bail out, 3. Bring forward, 4. Call for, 5. Draw on, 6. Fork out, 7. Hold off, 8. Nail down, 9. Run up, 10. Set aside

Detailed Explanations + Example Sentences:

1. Act on – To take action based on information or advice.

The manager acted on the client’s feedback to improve the product.

We need to act on this proposal quickly before our competitors.

He acted on his lawyer’s advice and settled the case.

2. Bail out – To provide financial assistance to prevent failure; to rescue.

The government bailed out the struggling bank during the crisis.

His parents bailed him out when he couldn't pay his rent.

Investors were hesitant to bail out the failing company.

3. Bring forward – To move an event to an earlier date/time.

The meeting was brought forward to accommodate the CEO’s schedule.

Can we bring forward the deadline to finish this by Friday?

They brought forward the product launch due to high demand.

4. Call for – To publicly demand or require something.

The situation calls for immediate action.

The employees called for better working conditions.

This task calls for someone with experience in finance.

5. Draw on – To make use of resources, skills, or experience.

She drew on her marketing experience to develop the campaign.

We can draw on our savings if cash flow becomes an issue.

He drew on his network to secure new business leads.

6. Fork out – To pay a large amount, often reluctantly.

We had to fork out $10,000 for new office equipment.

I don’t want to fork out for another expensive training course.

The company forked out a hefty sum to settle the dispute.

7. Hold off – To delay doing something.

We decided to hold off on hiring new staff until next quarter.

The launch was held off due to unforeseen issues.

She held off making a decision until more data was available.

8. Nail down – To finalize or confirm details.

We need to nail down the budget before proceeding.

After weeks of negotiation, they nailed down the contract.

Let’s nail down a date for the performance review meeting.

9. Run up – To accumulate (especially debts or costs).

He ran up a huge credit card bill during his trip.

The company ran up significant legal fees last year.

We’ve run up expenses beyond the approved budget.

10. Set aside – To reserve or save something (usually money or time).

We’ve set aside funds for employee training.

She set aside an hour every morning to check emails.

The company set aside resources for future expansion.
