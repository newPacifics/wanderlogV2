Count on, Deal with, Lean on, Touch base, Max out, Hammer out, Pull together, Sort out, Scope out, Knuckle down

1) Count on

Meaning: To rely on someone or something; to depend on a person, resource, or outcome.
Examples:
• We’re counting on you to finish the presentation by Friday.
• You can always count on your team for support during busy times.

2) Deal with

Meaning: To handle or manage a situation, problem, or set of tasks.
Examples:
• Please deal with the client’s complaint promptly.
• I need more training on how to deal with unexpected issues.

3) Lean on

Meaning: To depend or rely on someone for help or advice; sometimes also to put pressure on someone to act.

Examples:
• We can lean on the IT department if we have any technical problems.
• The supervisor leaned on the suppliers to lower their prices.

4) Touch base

Meaning: To briefly make contact or reconnect with someone, typically to update or get updated on a situation.
Examples:
• Let’s touch base later this week about the budget changes.
• I touched base with my manager to confirm the details of the new project.

5) Max out

Meaning: To reach the highest limit of something (e.g., budget, capacity, credit, effort).
Examples:
• We’re about to max out the travel budget if we book another flight.
• After two hours of calls, I realized I’d maxed out my phone battery.

6) Hammer out

Meaning: To work out a solution or agreement after detailed discussion or negotiation.
Examples:
• The two departments spent the afternoon hammering out the new marketing strategy.
• It took hours to hammer out all the details of the partnership agreement.

7) Pull together

Meaning: To collaborate and work harmoniously toward a common goal; also can mean to regain composure.
Examples:
• The team really pulled together to meet the project deadline.
• We need everyone to pull together when tackling these last-minute changes.

8) Sort out

Meaning: To organize, resolve, or clarify a confusing or complicated matter.
Examples:
• We should sort out the scheduling issues before hiring more staff.
• It took me the whole morning to sort out all those invoice errors.

9) Scope out

Meaning: To examine or investigate something carefully, often to assess feasibility, size, or potential.
Examples:
• We need to scope out the new office location before we sign a lease.
• Let’s scope out our competitors’ pricing strategies before we launch.

10) Knuckle down

Meaning: To begin to work seriously; to focus and put in hard effort.
Examples:
• I knuckled down this morning to finish that report.
• The whole team knuckled down when the project entered its final phase.
