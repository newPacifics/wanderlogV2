summary: >
  The *Eclectic Readers* series—often called *McGuffey’s Eclectic Readers*—was a
  foundational set of graded schoolbooks used throughout the 19th and early
  20th centuries in the United States. Each volume introduced reading skills
  progressively, combining vocabulary, phonics, moral lessons, short stories,
  and poetry. The series shaped American literacy education for generations and
  reflected the cultural values of its time, emphasizing character, discipline,
  and practical language learning.
